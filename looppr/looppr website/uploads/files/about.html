Our story

We built TidyFolds because
laundry was stealing our Sundays.

There are 35,000 laundromats in the US sitting half-empty on weekday mornings. And millions of people spending hours every week on a chore they hate. We connect them — and get out of the way.

How we started

A laundromat, a spreadsheet, and a borrowed van.

In the spring of 2024, our co-founder Alex was helping a friend move into a new apartment in Tulsa. The building had no in-unit laundry. The nearest laundromat was four blocks away but packed on weekends and empty on Tuesday mornings.

He started talking to the owner — a woman named Rosa who'd run the place for 22 years. She told him she'd love more business during slow hours but had no way to reach new customers. She'd tried a Yelp ad once. It didn't work.

That conversation led to a spreadsheet, then a group chat, then a borrowed van, then three test orders. Those three test orders turned into forty. Then four hundred. Then a company.

TidyFolds is built on one belief: the infrastructure already exists. The laundromats are there. The drivers are there. The customers are there. Someone just had to connect them properly — and make the experience good enough that people actually come back.

What we believe

Four things we won't compromise on

Local partners, not warehouses

Every order goes to a real laundromat in your neighborhood — a local business run by real people. We'll never build a central processing facility. The whole point is to make what's already there work better.

Drivers who earn a living wage

We guarantee a minimum of $18/hr for new drivers and structure our pay to reward quality. We're not racing to the bottom on driver pay. Good pay produces reliable drivers, which produces happy customers.

Honest turnaround times

We only promise what we can deliver. If your order is going to be late, we'll tell you — proactively, not after you've been waiting. We'd rather under-promise and over-deliver every time.

If something goes wrong, we fix it

No escalation queues. No "submit a ticket." If your order is damaged, late, or wrong, we respond the same day and make it right. Full stop. The platform liability policy covers up to $300 per order.

Why TidyFolds

We're not just another delivery app.

1

We use the neighborhood's existing laundromats

Other services build centralized facilities. We partner with the laundromat two blocks from you — which means faster turnaround, lower carbon footprint, and money staying local.

2

Your preferences are remembered forever

Most services treat every order as your first. We build a profile — detergent, water temp, fold style, allergens — and apply it automatically. The second order should feel better than the first.

3

We're transparent about what the driver earns

Tip 100% goes to your driver. We publish our minimum pay guarantee publicly. We think this matters — and we think customers deserve to know where their money goes.

4

We're local and intend to stay that way

We're growing city by city, intentionally. We don't expand until the operations in the prior city are genuinely good. Quality before coverage.

Local impact

Keeping dollars in the neighborhood.

Every TidyFolds order pays a local laundromat owner, employs a local driver, and saves a local customer time they can spend on anything else. We're not extracting value from a community — we're circulating it.

In our first pilot city, TidyFolds sent over $60,000 to local laundromat partners in under six months. That's money that stayed in Tulsa.

As we grow into Edmond and OKC, that's the number we track most carefully. Not just GMV. Not just orders. How much did local laundromat owners earn this month?
